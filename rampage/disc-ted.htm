    Vic's Thoughts on This End Down

    Despite taking 16 years to come to fruition, the idea stayed the same - Take some of what we thought were our best songs, knock out some quick rehearsal-style music, give it a vocal pass or two, and put it out there. Since we're not a live band, this is the closest thing we'll be able to put out in the vein of a live gig or live album, and the stripped-down approach gives a more honest view of the music and our interpretations of it. Also, given the depth of the back catalog compared to the amount that Aerik has sung on, I think it's interesting to hear what a real vocalist can do with what I write.

    Right about the time I was recording what would have been the Misogyny II EP and the rerecorded Bellum, I also knocked out some covers. I did them in two batches - four songs in my usual style, tuned down to D, focusing on the death/thrash side of things as I did with Death Beast and on This End Up. The inclusion of "Here Comes the War" was specifically requested by Aerik because he wanted to give his own lyrics a crack to see what he could do with it. The other four were more on the doom/epic side, and since I was tuning down an extra half-step for that EP I thought the lower tuning would fit some of the Monolith-era songs and "Displeasures..." pretty well. A Sabbath cover is also floating around out there from these same sessions, by the way...

    Anyway, I recorded these tracks at the end of 2005 and into early January 2006, then sent them to Aerik just before life split our paths in separate directions. We did try to reconvene a couple of times in the intervening years, but ultimately nothing came of it.

    Once Aerik and I reconnected and recommitted to work in early 2022, he asked for these tracks again. Since his studio is in-house now, he could work on them easily, and within two weeks of me giving him all of the music we had a finished rehearsal album.
