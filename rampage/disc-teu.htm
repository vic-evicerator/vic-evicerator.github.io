Vic's Thoughts on This End Up

When it comes to this album, I vacillate between guarded pride and wanting to issue a complete apology. Most of it was conceived while Rampage was still in the demo stage, yet the complete-band sound I got was miles beyond the old boombox demos and even the previous "Misogyny" Demo-EP sound, and it was the first really somewhat-coherent album I released. Some of it is juvenile, humorous (or silly, depending on how charitable a mood you're in), and more often than not the influences are on the sleeve. Still, at the time, I was very enthusiastic about it, and it WAS a growth beyond what had gone before.

I remember recording these songs in four distinct batches, and if you listen closely to the production sounds you can tell what is what. At the time, the PC I was using for recording was pretty limited disk-wise, so I had to do songs in batches of three or four, and then clean the disk when those were done to do the next set. Bloody Leg and Six Bells... were tracked at the end of the "Misogyny" sessions. If I remember correctly, the next batch was Born in Hell, Satanic Symphony, Satanic Death, and The Spectre were next - it was the dual-guitar work on all of those that I remember doing at the same time, not to mention the fact that I couldn't get a bass tone I was happy with for some reason - the bass on all of those tracks was pretty muddy and somewhat buried as a result. The next batch was Ticket to Hell, Gates of the Abyss, Heaven's Gate, Rampage, and Eye of the HellStorm. I remember this set because I was so unhappy with the bass on the last set that I worked hard to get a better tone that stood out more. Of course, Ticket... and Gates... have distorted bass as well, so that's how I remember that set. On the last stretch, the covers of Jailhouse Rock and Witches' Sabbath, my guitar amp died and I had to borrow someone else's amp. That's why the guitars on those two songs were so thin compared to the rest of the album.

Then, right as I was ready to mix the album, the power supply on my PC was hit by a power surge. and I had to wait three weeks to get a replacement. I was pretty nervous about that, since I saw that I was almost done recording at the beginning of October and thought that Halloween would make a perfect release date. Then the guitar amp problem happened, and I worked around it, then came the PC problem... Still, I just managed to pull it off. At the time I didn't master my own CDs (in fact, I had to do my mixdowns to cassette tape), so I was really in a rush when I was back up and running again - I mixed it pretty fast in a day or two and mailed the tape off to a studio called Crescent Digital to convert it to CDR.

Aerik's thoughts on This End Up:

Well, This End Up was the first Rampage recording I heard about two-three years ago and in many ways it is still the one I crawl back to whenever I'm in a mood to shatter some skulls. I remember the one-two punch of "Satanic Death" and "Bloody Leg" and only wondering how these tracks would come across had Vic NOT been the singer...even so, the strength of the melodies helped keep me in the listening game.

Not long after I had contacted Vic himself about possibly releasing Black Moon Rising material and a long-term friendship has since been building that has lead to my joining the band at this time. Overall, this record is the first, the most "rocking" and the one that convinced me that Vic was a largely "unsung hero" of the metal scene. (So funny, I made myself vomit with disgust, am I!)

If you haven't picked this up yet, the dual solos on "Satanic Death", the moody "Six Bells at Midnight" and the churning "Bloody Leg" still make regular rounds in my player...and will probably bear my voice at some point in the near future. "This End Up" may not be the most original (*cough*POSSESSED*cough*) record known to the history of man, but it certainly rates high on the "beat the fuck out of your stereo speakers" factor and has more hooks than in a horror movie with the victims finding themselves in a meat packing plant.

My hails to a great first LP...the one that got me interested in the first place...
