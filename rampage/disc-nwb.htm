Vic's Thoughts on New World Blasphemy

"Conceit" is now the way I'd characterize this release - the conceit that just by listening to something and understanding something that one can thereby DO that thing. For the past few years I had been listening to more and more black metal, and getting into more of those kind of bands, even getting a few of them onto UHR, so I thought that it would be easy for me to just write some black metal with Rampage.

Riffs and snippets of songs appeared here and there but nothing really inspired me - until I came across the intro riff to "Black Flames...". It almost wrote itself in a day or two. The next day (I confess, after listening to Barathrum's Eerie album...) I was toying with a more primitive ritualistic-type of feel, and the drumbeat and throbbing bass riff of "Ritual Curse" popped out of nowhere. Another day or two later that song was finished. I was far from ready to release an album, but I wanted to get these tracks out there. Luckily, at the time I had just gotten in contact with some raw, underground USBM bands (Song of Melkor, Chernobog, Cross Sodomy, Enbilulugugal...), and after some back-and-forths I managed to organize this split release with SOM and Cross Sodomy.

I got it organized, released, and then started working on the next album... when nothing happened. I started about 20 songs and abandoned them all... they were all too obvious, or too tired/trite sounding, or just plain cloned. Looking at it then, I thought it was just writer's block, and that continuing to 'work' on the problem would fix it. Now, though, I think I understand what writer's block really is - it's your subconscious telling you that something you're doing isn't what you really want or need to do.

I gave up, dejected at the time, and then Monolith... fell together. I still thought I could just 'clean the closet' and end that 'old chapter' of Rampage by getting Monolith out of my system. It fell together so easily that I should have suspected something, but at the time I didn't care - I was just happy that my plate was empty and I could start to 'really' work on the next Rampage 'black metal' album.

Of course, it didn't happen. Not immediately, anyway. Earlier, I thought that it was because I just wasn't a black metal musician - I couldn't reach that level of darkness I thought it needed. Crimson Frost proved that wasn't the case, and it was instead something else - I was too busy trying to capture what others did with Black Metal instead of just taking the evil vibe and looking for my own take on it. Fortunately Aerik helped me see that and together we realized it a few years after this release.

And we just might get back there again someday.
