Vic's Thoughts on Crimson Frost

Before updating this page I saw that my thoughts on this release were all about the hardships I had in writing and releasing it - the many abandoned and fruitless song attempts, my constant focus-shifting as what I wanted to do with this release kept changing depending on who I was listening to, and then the problematic release issues (with the label who solicited us to release it then dropped us after we kept having to talk them back into working with us...).

Rewriting this years later, though, I realized I had forgotten about most of this, and what remains now when I think of this release is how proud I am of what we accomplished here. Aerik really helped me focus on what makes black metal what it is and, in doing so, helped me strip away the parts that were in the way of us getting to that black, evil core of the music. It's also (so far) the only live-drum release I've ever done with Rampage, and overall one that I think has the most fitting production job I've ever put on one of our releases.

There are a couple of lessons from there that I think I've taken, and they'll be invaluable going forward. Time will tell, I suppose.
