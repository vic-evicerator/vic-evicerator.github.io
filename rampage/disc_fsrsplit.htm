Rampage "Doom Metal" recorded October-November 1998 in Bloody Leg Studios, Stockbridge GA. Produced by Lord Vic.

Vic's thoughts on Doom Metal:

The same day I released This End Up I started working on a cover of Venom's "Leave Me in Hell" for the UHR online Tribute that I had organized with Jason Christie of Gortician. I finished it and then a few days later decided to knock out a cover of Immortal's "Unsilent Storms..." just for fun. I posted it exclusively on the webpage at the time, but then pulled it down when I decided to put it on the split CD. "Doom Metal" was recorded right after the Immortal cover, about March or April 1999. At the time, I had intended to finish the Doom Metal album first and then record Bellum Infinitum, but after finishing this song I decided against it. As you can hear, I had improved the sounds I could get out of the studio quite a bit from the first demo, and I felt at the time I just couldn't wait any longer to hear the whole Bellum... album fully realized.

Since I decided to put this out quickly just to get it out of the way, I was hard-pressed to think of a way to do it, since three songs (two of them covers) is almost not worth the effort on its own. Fortunately, I had finally gotten a decent demo out of Festering Sore at the time, and with all of the splits going around the underground at the time I thought it was a natural move.
