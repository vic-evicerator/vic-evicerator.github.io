Vic's thoughts on Salomon's Gate:

This album was ChorazaiM's idea, basically. We were talking one day, the subject of the flood of tributes came up, and he noticed that nobody had done a Beherit tribute before - and then said that we should do one. He organized many of the bands, I did the rest, and we asked our little group of bands for someone who could do art to do the cover - that ended up being Eric M. Syre.

As for Rampage's own contribution, there's not much to it - I liked the Drawing Down the Moon album best, and knew that everyone would go for "The Gate of Nanna", so I figured something else was in order. Lyrics only exist for a handful of the tracks, and those had been earmarked for covers by other bands as well... I ended up picking Salomon's Gate because it was unpicked, because it was the opener on DDTM and thought it would be a good way to open this album as well, and because it had a lot of variation in tempo and structure throughout and thought it came closest to Rampage's style of music. As for the lyrics, I tried to make them out as best I could and then was forced to fill in the rest John-Tardy-style.
