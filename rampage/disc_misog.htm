Vic's thoughts on Misogyny, Thy Name is Woman:

This EP was the first "full-band" demo, and since I was still learning how to record and use the studio software it shows in the production. It's the least serious in terms of songs, so that turns off some metal fans or gives the impression that everything by Rampage was less-than-serious. Still, I have a soft spot for this demo since it was the first thing I ever fully recorded on my own, and it was gratifying to hear that I could make one person could sound like a full band.

The songs are mostly 'odds and ends', since I wrote most of them at very different times and they were really just the songs that didn't fit anywhere else (they weren't satanic/death metal enough for This End Up, doomy enough for Doom Metal, and weren't part of the story of Bellum Infinitum). The only exception, of course, is "Bloody Leg". I had finished recording the other six songs and had started working on two more to get a head start on This End Up - by the time I was mixing "Bloody Leg" had already been finished so I decided to throw it on as a teaser for the upcoming album.

Am I embarrassed by it now? Yes. Do I wish it had never gotten out? I'm not sure. I guess in an ideal world I would have people take it as a different band (which it was, in the way it was written anyway), but I'm neurotic about having "Rampage" appear as the name on anything I do with music, and I guess I trust too much that people can see something that is done as a joke as a joke in itself without really looking at the whole band as something less than serious. Ah well, I dug my grave, now I'll lie in it. And at least chuckle at the bitches who inspired all this... fuck it, I'll blame it on them - it's their fault anyway. They ruined my life then, and they ruined my music too. Bitches....

Aerik's thoughts on Misogyny, Thy Name is Woman:

This second demo I found to be quite fun, but utterly pointless to Vic's artists designs. I love the riffs and the silly put-on "teenage anger" lyrics for what they are...but really, Vic had such better material in his system that I don't think this is worth chasing after unless you are like me...the Rampage diehard fanatic of doooooom! (...Or not...)
